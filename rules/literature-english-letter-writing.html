Letter Writing

Literature — English

Back to Literature — English General Guidelines Rulebook PDF

Topics

  • A letter to the silence that spoke louder than words.
  • A letter to the apology you never gave and explain what held you back.
  • A letter to a teacher who changed your way of thinking.
  • A letter to your father expressing what you’ve never said aloud.
  • A letter to your mother thanking her for her silent sacrifices.

Rules and Guidelines

  • Word count: between 500 and 800 words.
  • Candidates must mention the selected topic in their submission.
  • Correct letter formatting does not carry additional marks; it must clearly state the addressee.
  • Judging will consider content, language, coherence, and relevance to the chosen topic.
  • Good grammar and the use of literary devices will carry additional marks.
